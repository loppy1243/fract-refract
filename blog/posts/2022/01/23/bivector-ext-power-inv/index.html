    Inverses of Exterior Powers of a Bivector

    [1] https://arxiv.org/abs/2107.03771v1

    In their recent paper [1], Roelfs and De Keninck present a very intriguing coordinate-free decomposition of any bivector BBB into a sum of a particular class of simple bivectors for any Clifford algebra over a real vector space VVV. In their proof, it's necessary at the final step to invert the multivector DDD where

    D:={λr−1W1+λr−2W3+⋯+Wk−1if k is even,λrW0+λr−1W2+⋯+Wk−1if k is odd, D := \begin{cases} \lambda^{r-1}W_1 + \lambda^{r-2}W_3 + \cdots + W_{k-1} &\text{if }k\text{ is even}, \\ \lambda^rW_0 + \lambda^{r-1}W_2 + \cdots + W_{k-1} &\text{if }k\text{ is odd}, \end{cases} D:={λr−1W1​+λr−2W3​+⋯+Wk−1​λrW0​+λr−1W2​+⋯+Wk−1​​if k is even,if k is odd,​ Wm:=1m!B∧B∧⋯∧B⏟m times, W_m := \frac1{m!}\underbrace{B\wedge B\wedge\cdots\wedge B}_{m\text{ times}}, Wm​:=m!1​m timesB∧B∧⋯∧B​​, n=dim⁡(V),k=⌊n/2⌋,r=⌊k/2⌋, n = \dim(V),\quad k = \lfloor n/2\rfloor,\quad r = \lfloor k/2\rfloor, n=dim(V),k=⌊n/2⌋,r=⌊k/2⌋,

    and λ\lambdaλ is a particular constant. However, they don't give any indication as to why this multivector is invertible.

    As a first step, we can try finding the inverse of

    Bm:=B∧B∧⋯∧B⏟m times, \mathcal B_m := \underbrace{B\wedge B\wedge\cdots\wedge B}_{m\text{ times}}, Bm​:=m timesB∧B∧⋯∧B​​,

    and all of us would feel warm and fuzzy inside if it were

    Bm−1=?Am:=B−1∧B−1⋯∧B−1⏟m times, \mathcal B_m^{-1} \overset?= A_m := \underbrace{B^{-1}\wedge B^{-1}\cdots\wedge B^{-1}}_{m\text{ times}}, Bm−1​=?Am​:=m timesB−1∧B−1⋯∧B−1​​,

    and it is!

    ...maybe. I'll show that when Bm−1\mathcal B_m^{-1}Bm−1​ does exist, then it must be AmA_mAm​. But it's not clear to me how to show that Bm−1\mathcal B_m^{-1}Bm−1​ must exist. Perhaps I'm just missing something basic here, though.
