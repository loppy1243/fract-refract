    Field Norms

    In reading Larry Groves book Classical Groups and Geometric Algebra[2], I ended up needing to review (well, really learn about) norms of field extensions. Finding their properities listed somewhere was easy enough, but proofs of these properties seemed hard to come by or were relegated to exercises. So, I resolved to figure it out myself. Perhaps these proofs are standard, but I found them interesting enough to want to share here.

    Many of the necessary little field theory facts are taken from Dummit & Foote[1].

    Norm as a Determinant

    We will only consider finite field extensions throughout. Some of this may be applicable in more generality, but I don't really care right now.

    For given a field extension L/KL/KL/K, we can notice that x↦axx \mapsto axx↦ax for a,x∈La, x \in La,x∈L is a KKK-linear transformation. It seems natural then to consider the determinant of this,

    NL/K(a)=det⁡ ⁣K(x↦ax), N_{L/K}(a) = \det\!{}_K(x \mapsto ax), NL/K​(a)=detK​(x↦ax),

    and we'll call this the norm of aaa in L/KL/KL/K. From this definition, we can see immediately that for any k∈Kk \in Kk∈K and b∈Lb \in Lb∈L,

    NL/K(k)=k[L:K],NL/K(ab)=NL/K(a)NL/K(b). N_{L/K}(k) = k^{[L:K]},\quad N_{L/K}(ab) = N_{L/K}(a)N_{L/K}(b). NL/K​(k)=k[L:K],NL/K​(ab)=NL/K​(a)NL/K​(b).

    Norm in an Extension Tower

    At least when the degree of ppp is [L:K][L:K][L:K] we have a recipe to compute the norm of aaa: it's the product of the roots of ppp (in a field extension where it splits).     This comes from the observation that you can calculate the determinant by performing Gaussian elimination on such a matrix block-wise, essentially treating the blocks like scalars. Notice for example that multiplying the first block-row of a block-matrix XXX by a block AAA (to get the block-matrix YYY) is the same as multiplying XXX on the left by the block-matrix ZZZ which has AAA in the top-left corner and ones along the rest of the diagonal; then det⁡(Z)=det⁡(A)\det(Z) = \det(A)det(Z)=det(A) and so det⁡(Y)=det⁡(ZX)=det⁡(A)det⁡(X)\det(Y) = \det(ZX) = \det(A)\det(X)det(Y)=det(ZX)=det(A)det(X), just as if AAA was a "scalar". A full exposition takes us too far from the main topic, so we won't go into it here.

    where A,B,C,DA, B, C, DA,B,C,D are appropriate matrices. The punchline is then

    det⁡ ⁣K([b]M/K)=det⁡ ⁣K([det⁡ ⁣L([b]M/L)]L/K). \det\!{}_K([b]_{M/K}) = \det\!{}_K\Bigl(\bigl[\det\!{}_L([b]_{M/L})\bigr]_{L/K}\Bigr). detK​([b]M/K​)=detK​([detL​([b]M/L​)]L/K​).

    We take the block-wise determinant of [b]M/K[b]_{M/K}[b]M/K​ to get [det⁡ ⁣L([b]M/L)]L/K[\det\!{}_L([b]_{M/L})]_{L/K}[detL​([b]M/L​)]L/K​ since the blocks represent elements of LLL, then det⁡ ⁣K([b]M/K)\det\!{}_K([b]_{M/K})detK​([b]M/K​) is the determinant of this by the "determinant of the determinant of blocks" property.

    Norm in a Galois Extension

    In the case that L/KL/KL/K is Galois, we get a particularly nice form for the norm. Since we're in a Galois extension, ppp splits, it's roots are distinct, and they are exactly given by the set

    {σ(a) ∣ σ∈Gal(L/K)}. \{\sigma(a) \;\mid\; \sigma \in \mathrm{Gal}(L/K)\}. {σ(a)∣σ∈Gal(L/K)}.

    Note, crucially, that there may be more than one element of Gal(L/K)\mathrm{Gal}(L/K)Gal(L/K) that maps aaa to a particular root. What we will do is count these repetitions.

    Let H⊆Gal(L/K)H \subseteq \mathrm{Gal}(L/K)H⊆Gal(L/K) be the stabilizer of a.a.a. Then for any σ,σ′\sigma, \sigma'σ,σ′ in the same coset of H,H,H,

    σ−1σ′∈(σ−1σ′)H=σ−1(σ′H)=σ−1(σH)=H \sigma^{-1}\sigma' \in (\sigma^{-1}\sigma')H = \sigma^{-1}(\sigma'H) = \sigma^{-1}(\sigma H) = H σ−1σ′∈(σ−1σ′)H=σ−1(σ′H)=σ−1(σH)=H ⟹ (σ−1σ′)(a)=a ⟹ σ′(a)=σ(a). \implies (\sigma^{-1}\sigma')(a) = a \implies \sigma'(a) = \sigma(a). ⟹(σ−1σ′)(a)=a⟹σ′(a)=σ(a).

    Thus every coset of HHH corresponds exactly to the distinct values that Gal(L/K)\mathrm{Gal}(L/K)Gal(L/K) can map aaa to. The field fixed by HHH is K(a),K(a),K(a), so since this is a Galois extension the size and number of the cosets are

    ∣H∣=[L:K(a)],∣G/H∣=[K(a):L]. |H| = [L:K(a)],\quad |G/H| = [K(a):L]. ∣H∣=[L:K(a)],∣G/H∣=[K(a):L].

    This is all to say that, for Gal(L/K)={1,σ1,σ2,…,σn},\mathrm{Gal}(L/K) = \{1, \sigma_1, \sigma_2,\dotsc, \sigma_n\},Gal(L/K)={1,σ1​,σ2​,…,σn​}, the sequence a,σ1(a),σ2(a),…,σn(a)a, \sigma_1(a), \sigma_2(a), \dotsc, \sigma_n(a)a,σ1​(a),σ2​(a),…,σn​(a) repeats each of the [K(a):L]=∣G/H∣[K(a):L] = |G/H|[K(a):L]=∣G/H∣ distinct roots of ppp exactly ∣H∣=[L:K(a)]|H| = [L:K(a)]∣H∣=[L:K(a)] times.     NL/K(a)=∏σ∈Gal(L/K)σ(a). N_{L/K}(a) = \prod_{\sigma \in \mathrm{Gal}(L/K)}\sigma(a). NL/K​(a)=σ∈Gal(L/K)∏​σ(a).

    Summary

    • The field norm is a multiplicative function, and a power function over the base field with power the index of the extension.

    • The norm over a tower of field extensions is the composition of norms going down the tower.

    • The norm is always the product of all the roots of the minimal polynomial p,p,p, all raised to the index/ ⁣deg⁡(p)\text{index}/\!\deg(p)index/deg(p) power.

    • In a Galois extension, the norm is the product of all the Galois conjugates.
