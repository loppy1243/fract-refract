    Notation and Definitions

    Geometric/Clifford algebra

    • A ⌋ BA \mkern3mu{\rfloor}\mkern3mu BA⌋B and A ⌊ BA \mkern3mu{\lfloor}\mkern3mu BA⌊B. These are, respectively, the left and right contractions, which are a much more natural and intuitive way of handling metric operations as opposed to using some sort of symmetric dot product. [1] L. Dorst, The Inner Products of Geometric Algebra See [1].

    • A∗B=⟨AB⟩0,A * B = \langle AB\rangle_0,A∗B=⟨AB⟩0​, the scalar part of the geometric product.

    • A⊆B.A \subseteq B.A⊆B. This means that x∧A=0x \wedge A = 0x∧A=0 implies x∧B=0x \wedge B = 0x∧B=0 for all vectors xxx. This makes the most sense for blades, since they more clearly represent particular subspaces.
